3. Summary — in one minute

We hold account and authentication data on a remote database (Firestore). All health and fitness data are stored only locally on the user's device (SQLite) and are not uploaded to Firestore or any remote servers by default. Users may export their app data as a CSV file from inside the app. Local health data are removed automatically when the app is uninstalled or when the app's cache/data is cleared.

4. What personal data we collect

We collect categories of personal data necessary to provide and improve the Services. These include:

  • Identifiers & contact (stored on Firestore): email, username. If you sign-in via Google or Apple, we store provider identifiers necessary to manage the account. We also store basic account attributes such as credit/account type (e.g., "free", "premium").
  • Authentication data (stored on Firestore): authentication-related records needed to let you sign in (for example email/password auth metadata or provider tokens/identifiers). Note: exact token formats and how passwords are handled depend on your chosen authentication implementation; contact support for details.
  • Payment & billing (where applicable): records related to purchases or subscriptions; payment processing is handled by platform billing providers (Google Play Billing, Apple In-App Purchase). We do not store full card numbers on our servers.
  • Device & technical data: device identifiers, OS/version, crash logs, performance metrics.
  • Usage & analytics: app usage events and aggregated analytics identifiers (only where enabled/consented).
  • Health & fitness data — STORED LOCALLY ONLY (SQLite): the specific health-related data we collect and store locally on your device includes:
    • Food and drink you consume and calorie counts
    • Fitness level, activity and goals (e.g., weekly goals or habits like “eat more protein”)
    • Body measurements (height, weight, BMI)
    • Progress photos you choose to provide
    • Workout history, heart rate or other biometric metrics you voluntarily provide

    Important: the health & fitness data above remain on your device in a local SQLite database by default and are not uploaded to Firestore or other remote servers unless you explicitly use an app feature that sends them (for example, a manual export or an opt-in backup).

5. Local storage, Firestore and what is sent to the cloud

Local storage (SQLite): health & fitness data (listed above) are stored in a local SQLite database inside the app on your device. These data are not transmitted to our remote database or to third parties by default.

Firestore (remote): We use a remote database (Google Cloud Firestore) to store account-level data required for account management and cross-device sign-in. Only the following categories are stored on Firestore by default:

  • Email and username
  • Authentication related records (provider identifiers for Google Sign-In or Apple Sign-In; and metadata for email/password sign-in)
  • Account metadata such as account/credit type (e.g., free, premium)
  • Minimal flags or pointers to manage sync/status (but not health details)

All other health & fitness data remain local unless you explicitly export or opt-in to a backup feature.

6. Local data deletion & uninstall behavior

Local health & fitness data stored in SQLite are deleted automatically when either:

  1. You uninstall the app from your device (the operating system removes app data); or
  2. You clear the app's data/cache via your device settings (this removes the local SQLite database and associated local files).

If you delete your account via the app, we will remove your Firestore account records (email, username, account metadata) according to our deletion policy. Because health data are local, account deletion does not by itself remove health data stored locally unless the app triggers local deletion on your device as part of the account deletion flow — check the in-app settings or contact support to request device-side deletion instructions.

7. Exporting your data (CSV)

The app provides an export feature that allows you to create a CSV file containing your data from the app (including health & fitness entries you have stored locally). When you create/export a CSV:

  • The CSV file is generated on your device and saved according to your device's file-handling (for example, Downloads folder). We do not automatically upload that CSV to our servers.
  • Once exported, the CSV file is under your control — we recommend you store it securely because anyone with access to that file can read the exported data.
  • If you choose to share or upload the CSV to cloud storage or email it, those actions are outside our control and subject to those third-party services' privacy practices.

8. Backups & device sync

Local SQLite data may be included in your device's automatic backups (for example, Google Drive backups on Android or iCloud backups on iOS) if you have backups enabled. If you do not want your app data included in device backups, disable the device backup feature or follow device-specific instructions. We do not control the contents of device backups and recommend you review your device backup settings.
